Life Is Surprising

I've struggled greatly with mental health a lot for most of life. I hit a new a low last November. I was going through it bad. Then one day while visiting my dad I got a call. My best friend, Cody, had to get rid of his dog. Now I had known Cody for at least a year and this had to happen for a multitude of reasons. I gladly took in this puppy. I already loved him so much. I now found myself with this sweet sensitive pup that was also going through it bad and I had a choice either step up or let this innocent creature down. He pushed me to want more. I wanted to get him the life he deserved. Now we went through our fair share of bumps in the beginning.

D-Bo's Suffers From:

  • Separation Anxiety

  • PTSD

  • Abandonment Issues

  • Nightmares

Now the first night was rough. If I'm completely honest neither of us slept that night; every noise scared him. It definitely didn't help that I lived on the second floor in a studio. He was a trooper though and in time we made progress. He still acts surprised every time I come home. I still wake him up when he whines, cries, and yelps in his sleep only to hold him while talking him down. He still jumps when anyone picks up a broom or makes too sudden of a move. If I'm gone for too long he acts out. After everything he's been through though we are making progress.

"Go and love someone exactly as they are. And watch how quickly they transform into the greatest, truest version of themselves. When one feels seen and appreciated in their own essence, one is empowered." -Wes Angelozzi
